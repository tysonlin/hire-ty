# Content Guard Rails

All CV and cover letter content generated by this agent **must** comply with the following rules.
These rules apply to every agent in `/subagents/` and to all final documents.

---

## Rule 1 — Strict factual accuracy

- Every claim, achievement, role, date, company name, technology, and qualification **must be sourced directly from `/data/tyson/`**.
- Do **not** invent, embellish, or infer facts that are not explicitly stated in the source files.
- If a piece of information is missing from `/data/tyson/`, leave a clearly marked placeholder (e.g. `[TODO: add X]`) rather than fabricating content.
- Numbers and metrics (years of experience, team sizes, revenue figures, performance improvements) must match exactly what is recorded in `/data/tyson/`.

---

## Rule 2 — Concise, simple language

- Write at a professional but accessible level. Aim for clarity over complexity.
- Prefer short sentences and active voice.
- Each bullet point in a CV should be a single, complete thought — no run-on sentences.
- Cover letters should be no longer than four paragraphs.
- Avoid jargon unless it is standard in the target industry and appears in the job description.
- Remove filler words: "very", "really", "basically", "quite", "in order to", "it is important to note that", etc.

---

## Rule 3 — No AI-sounding language

The following words, phrases, and patterns are **banned** from all output:

### Banned words & phrases
- delve, delving
- leverage, leveraging (prefer "use", "apply", or "build on")
- synergy, synergistic
- paradigm shift
- game-changer, game-changing
- cutting-edge (prefer "modern" or name the specific technology)
- world-class
- dynamic (as a vague descriptor)
- robust (when used vaguely — it is fine for specific technical contexts)
- holistic
- transformative
- harness, harnessing
- spearhead, spearheaded (prefer "led", "initiated")
- comprehensive (when used as vague filler)
- utilize, utilization (prefer "use")
- facilitate (prefer "help", "enable", "support")
- "I am passionate about…" (state the interest directly instead)
- "I am excited to…" (state the action or goal instead)
- Any em-dash (—) used as a stylistic flourish in body text
- Bullet points that start with "Responsible for" (prefer action verbs)
- Oxford-comma abuse that obscures meaning

### Banned notation & formatting
- Emoji in CVs or cover letters
- Bold text used for emphasis inside paragraphs (bold is for headings/labels only)
- Triple asterisks or other Markdown decoration in the final text
- Numbered lists in cover letter body paragraphs

---

## Rule 4 — Tone and register

- CVs must use **third-person implied** style (no "I" or "my" in bullet points).
- Cover letters must use **first-person** throughout.
- Match the tone to the seniority level and culture signalled by the job description (the Tone Optimizer sub-agent handles this).
- Do not use informal contractions in CVs (e.g. write "do not" not "don't").
- Contractions are acceptable in cover letters if the job description itself uses a conversational tone.

---

## Rule 5 — Structure and format

- CVs must follow the Typst template defined in `/Typst.md`.
- Cover letters must follow the email format template defined in `/subagents/tone-optimizer.md`.
- Section headings in CVs must not be changed arbitrarily — any structural changes must go through the Conflict Resolver sub-agent.
